For members

Getting care with GENFIN, step by step

You shouldn't need insurance expertise to use your medical aid. This is how your cover works in real life — when you need a doctor, medication, glasses or simply an answer.

When you need care

Four steps, handled for you

01

See a provider

Visit any GP, dentist, hospital or optician in the GENFIN network — or our own clinic at 89 Fife Avenue, Harare.

02

We pre-authorise

Where authorisation is needed, we confirm what's covered before treatment — so there are no surprises afterwards.

03

Receive your care

Consultations, treatment, spectacles or medication. At participating pharmacies, your fingerprint confirms your identity.

04

We settle directly

GENFIN pays the provider under managed care. You don't pay upfront and wait for a refund.

Your membership, day to day

The four things members use most

01

Pre-authorisation

Ask your provider to request authorisation, or contact us directly on WhatsApp or by phone. We confirm cover before treatment, often within minutes for routine care.

Before planned treatment
02

Claims

Most claims are settled directly with the provider, so there's nothing for you to submit. Where you do pay first, send us the invoice and we take it from there.

Settled with providers directly
03

Medication deliveries

Repeat chronic medication is prepared and delivered anywhere in Zimbabwe — to your home, school or workplace — and handed over against your delivery PIN.

Nationwide, PIN-confirmed
04

Dependants

Add your spouse and children to your membership so the whole family is covered under one plan, one premium and one point of contact.

One plan for the family
Chronic medication

From repeat script to your doorstep

Once your chronic prescription is on file, you don't chase it every month — we prepare your medication and deliver it across the country, to homes, schools and companies.

Every delivery is confirmed with a PIN at handover, so your medication reaches you and no one else. If anything changes — your address, your dosage, your timing — one message on WhatsApp updates it.
